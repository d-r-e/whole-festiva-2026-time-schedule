TICKETING

01How do Community Tickets and FLINTA* ticket options work (launching Jan 29)?

Community Tickets and FLINTA* Community Tickets for WHOLE 2026 launched online on Thursday, January 29 at 4:00 PM CET*

Community Tickets are a part of our ongoing commitment to do what we can to offer better access, care, and equity. We set these tickets aside to support people who face systemic, financial, or social barriers to accessing large-scale cultural events. They are an equity tool, not simply a lower price tier, and help keep WHOLE accessible and sustainable year after year.

This system is based on trust and self-selection:

  • Self-selection based on need
  • We don’t ask you to prove your need or identity
  • Distribution guided by equity & trust

Community Tickets (These are now sold out)
These are offered at a reduced price for people who need financial support to attend WHOLE. We ask everyone to self-select with intention. If you are able to regularly meet your financial needs, we kindly encourage you to purchase a regular-priced ticket so these options remain available to those who need them most.

FLINTA* Community Tickets (Still available)

FLINTA* Community Tickets are reserved for women, lesbians, intersex, non-binary, trans, and agender people who need financial support to attend WHOLE. These tickets exist to help create more equitable access for people that are historically marginalized in music and nightlife spaces. We ask applicants to self-select thoughtfully and with care.


Weekend Ticket – FLINTA-Focused* (Still available)
Reserved for women, lesbians, intersex, non-binary, trans, and agender people. This tier helps create intentional space for people historically marginalized in music and nightlife. By selecting this ticket, you confirm that you identify within the FLINTA* spectrum. This ticket is offered at a slightly reduced price (compared to the Regular Weekend Ticket).

02What other ticket tiers are available on Jan 29, and who are they for?

Alongside Community and FLINTA* Community Tickets (see above question), a limited number of additional ticket tiers will be available for purchase on January 29.

These include:

  • Regular Weekend Ticket

The standard ticket for attending WHOLE. Intended for people whose basic needs are met and who have financial stability. Purchasing a Regular Ticket directly supports the festival and helps make Community Tickets possible.

  • Weekend Ticket – FLINTA-Focused*

Reserved for women, lesbians, intersex, non-binary, trans, and agender people. This ticket helps create intentional space for genders historically marginalized in music and nightlife. This tier is not need-based and is offered at a slightly reduced rate compared to the Regular Weekend Ticket.

  • Supporter Weekend Ticket

For those with the financial capacity to give more. Supporter Tickets directly help fund Community Tickets and accessibility initiatives across the festival.

  • Sunday Ticket

For those who can’t attend the full weekend. Sunday Tickets are valid from Sunday morning onward.

Important note on ticket selection

  • All tickets in this release are limited in quantity.
  • If Regular Weekend Tickets sell out, we kindly ask that you do not resort to purchasing discounted ticket tiers (such as Community Tickets or FLINTA*-focused tickets) unless you genuinely fit the criteria they are intended for.
  • Please do not panic purchase. A next and final ticket release is planned for March, and more Regular Weekend Tickets will become available then. This system relies on trust and collective responsibility to remain fair and accessible for everyone.
  • If you plan to come by caravan or car, please make sure to purchase one of those ASAP, as they will sell out eventually.
  • If you plan to come by Bassliner bus, secure those tickets now, as they will sell out.

07How can I avoid buying a fake ticket?

Please only buy resale tickets via our trusted partner Tixel, or directly from someone you personally know and trust.

As WHOLE has grown, we’ve seen an increase in fraudulent resale attempts on social media. Unfortunately, tickets purchased from unknown or unverified sellers cannot be supported at the gate.

10Can I get a refund if I can’t attend?

Unfortunately not. All ticket sales are final. Like most large-scale festivals, ticket sales directly fund the production of the event — including infrastructure, staffing, and artist fees — which are committed many months in advance. For this reason, refunds are not possible once tickets are purchased.

11Can I resell my ticket?

Yes. For 2026, WHOLE works with Tixel as our official ticket resale partner.

Resale via Tixel is designed to be fair, transparent, and non-speculative. Tickets can be resold at a fixed price set by WHOLE, aligned with our regular ticket pricing at the time resale opens. This approach helps prevent price gouging, discourages profiteering, and keeps resale accessible to the wider community.

Using Tixel means:

  • Sellers can pass on their ticket through a secure, official channel
  • Buyers are protected from scams and extreme price inflation
  • Community and lower-priced tickets are not turned into profit opportunities

Resale via Tixel is the only officially supported resale method. We strongly discourage buying or selling tickets via social media, DMs, or unofficial platforms, as we cannot support those tickets at the gate.

Ticket resale will open no later than June 2026.

ACCESSIBILITY

01Is WHOLE accessible for wheelchair users? Are there any accessibility limitations on site?

We aim to make WHOLE as accessible as possible, while being transparent about the site’s limitations.

Accessible camping is located in the main campground and includes barrier-free toilets and showers, as well as close proximity to wheelchair-accessible shuttle stops. The on-site festival shuttle runs most of the weekend and is wheelchair accessible.

That said, WHOLE takes place on a former industrial site with uneven terrain. Some areas — especially sandy beaches, natural lakeshores, and certain paths on the peninsula (stage area) — are not fully wheelchair accessible. Accessibility can also vary depending on weather and ground conditions.

Please also note that Ferropolis does not allow assistance dogs or any animals on-site, due to high sound levels and overall site regulations.

02Is there a shuttle service on-site?

Our free on-site shuttle is wheelchair accessible and runs throughout the weekend, connecting Car Parking, Fußgängerbrücke (Footbridge) / Glamping, the Main Entrance, and the Peninsula.

Shuttle service operates as follows:

  • From Friday 09:30, two shuttle buses run between Car Parking, Fußgängerbrücke / Glamping, and the Main Entrance.
  • From Friday 14:00, the route is extended to include the Peninsula (stage area).
  • Shuttle service continues throughout the weekend, with additional capacity during peak arrival, departure, and nighttime hours.
  • At least one shuttle operates continuously until Monday 18:00.
  • The shuttle is provided free of charge. While we do our best to keep service running smoothly, occasional short pauses may be necessary for refueling, driver breaks, or operational needs. We appreciate your patience and understanding.

03Are Bassliner shuttles wheelchair accessible?

Bassliner shuttle routes from Dessau and Gräfenhainichen are wheelchair accessible.

For all other routes, foldable wheelchairs can usually be placed in the luggage compartment, depending on space availability. We recommend contacting Bassliner Support before booking to confirm arrangements.

Email: buero@bassliner.org

04Do you offer a companion pass?

If you have a disability card and have purchased a ticket, you’re welcome to write to us — we’re happy to extend one free companion pass to your accompanying person to help you out.

05Who can I contact with access-related questions?

For any accessibility-related questions or specific needs, please contact:

access@wholefestival.com

Reaching out by June is especially helpful, as it gives us enough time to prepare and accommodate you as best as possible.

06What if I need to bring medication or it needs to be refrigerated?

Please bring all medication in its original packaging. If you are carrying controlled substances, injectable medication (such as insulin or epinephrine), or larger quantities of medication, we recommend bringing a doctor's note or medical certificate to help avoid delays during security checks.

If you require refrigerated medication, please visit the Info Point at campsite upon arrival. Please clearly label all items with your name and contact information.

07How do I check in if I need accessibility support?

If you're arriving by Bassliner or taxi, go to the guestlist check-in at the main guest entrance to collect your wristband. Our Awareness Team (wearing pink vests) or security staff can help you avoid queues and guide you through the process.

08Is accessible parking available? Can I park my car directly at the accessible camping area?

Is accessible parking available?

Yes. Reserved accessible parking spaces are available in both the Car Parking area and the Caravan Sleeping Area.

Can I park my car directly at the accessible camping area?

No. Cars cannot remain parked at the accessible camping area. However, you can drive there to unload. Please inform security at the caravan entrance that you need access to the accessible camping area. You will be escorted there and can then park in the nearby accessible parking area (approximately 120 meters away).

09 Are there quiet or low-stimulation spaces available?

Please be aware that some festival areas may feature flashing/strobe lights, intense sound systems, or visual effects. Whether you need a sensory break, a place to decompress, or prefer a substance-free space, we’ve created several areas designed for calm and clarity:

Quiet Camping Area: If you prefer a low-noise sleeping environment, find the Quiet Camping Area on the map.

Sober Space: A dedicated area for folks who are sober, sober-curious, or simply want to take a break from substances.

Awareness Tents: Our Awareness tents offer a low-stimulation environment, stim toys and calming items for neurodivergent attendees, soft lighting and quiet seating areas as well as support staff if you need to talk or decompress.

10Can I charge my wheelchair, CPAP machine, or other medical equipment?

If you need to charge a wheelchair, CPAP machine, or other medically necessary equipment during the festival, please get in touch with the Accessibility Team at access@wholefestival.com before your arrival.

We will do our best to find a suitable solution.

11Are assistance/service dogs allowed? No animals are allowed on site.

Due to the high level noise on the festival site, Ferropolis does not allow animals on site.

12 Can friends camp with me in the accessible camping area?

Yes. Friends, partners, and support persons are welcome to camp with you in the accessible camping area.

However, we kindly ask everyone staying in this area to be mindful of the needs of other campers by:

  • Leaving the front rows closest to the facilities for wheelchair users and people with limited mobility
  • Keeping pathways clear of tents and cords
  • Maintaining a quieter atmosphere, especially during nighttime hours, for those who rely on a low-stimulation environment

SOLIDARITY PROGRAM

01What is the Solidarity Program and who is it for?

The Solidarity Program is for members of the queer community who are most impacted by systemic and economic inequalities and might not otherwise be able to attend WHOLE. Our aim is to support people who experience structural marginalization in multiple ways.

We are prioritizing applications from refugees, Black and POC folks, trans and gender-expansive people, people with disabilities, and others whose bodies are marginalized or excluded. You do not need to fit every category listed to apply.

Due to limited funding, the program is currently only available to residents of EU countries. We hope in the future to extend the program to queer folks around the world.

Applications for 2026 Solidarity Program are now closed.

02What does the Solidarity Program include?

Awardees will receive:

  • A festival ticket
  • Bus tickets to and from the festival (via Bassliner)
  • A €100 food and drink allowance
  • Camping gear for participants traveling from outside of Germany

Additional travel support:

  • €250 travel contribution for those traveling from outside Germany
  • €50 travel contribution for those traveling within Germany but outside Bassliner departure cities

    Applications for 2026 Solidarity Program are now closed.

03How does the application and selection process work?

We’ve reached the maximum number of applications, and the Solidarity Program application for WHOLE 2026 is now closed. Thank you to everyone who applied and took the time to share their experiences with us. The application form is no longer available online.

The application asked for details about applicants and their financial situation. Demographic questions help us understand applicants’ experiences of structural oppression so we can distribute support as equitably as possible.

A jury of four members from Berlin’s queer community will review the applications anonymously (names are removed). Decisions are based on applicants’ written answers, with the aim of supporting those for whom this initiative makes WHOLE more accessible.

For 2026, we are offering 100 Solidarity Awards. The selection process is based on trust. All applicants will receive a response by 2 April 2026.

Thank you again to everyone who applied.

CAMPING

01General info

  • Camping is included with your ticket. You’ll need to bring or rent your own setup.
  • The main campground is separate from the stage area. Expect a 15+ minute walk from the campground entrance to the peninsula (stages). The free on-site festival shuttle will return & run mostly all weekend.
  • A designated Quiet Camp area is sectioned off in the back corner for those looking to catch some ZZZs.
  • Please be mindful of noise at night. No Bluetooth parties. Please save loud music for the dancefloors.
  • The best life is camp life. Bring snacks to share, decorate your space, and get to know the people around you. Festival magic happens in the small moments, and a little effort goes a long way.

Facilities

  • All camping facilities are located within the main campground, including toilets and additional Dixi toilets across the site, showers (€4.50, cashless payment), large sink stations, the legendary Douche Station (€6.00, cashless payment), and multiple water refill points.

Shower and Douche Station fees help cover the cost of renting the facilities, installing temporary plumbing and water infrastructure, and maintaining them throughout the festival.

Please note that with thousands of guests using the showers, especially during peak hours, hot water cannot be guaranteed. If a hot shower is important to you, we recommend avoiding the busiest morning and evening periods.

  • Might we suggest bringing your own toilet paper, soap, and hand sanitizer. We do our best to restock and support facilities in real time, but it’s always helpful to be a little self-sufficient, as we can’t provide an endless supply.
  • Fun fact: Last year we went through an absolutely unhinged amount of toilet paper — the kind usually seen at festivals three times our size. We stock and regularly restock toilet paper throughout the weekend, but with thousands of people on site there may be moments when supplies temporarily run low. Bringing a spare roll for your camp is a small move that can save the day.

02Accessibility & Barrier-Free Camping

  • Accessible camping is located in the main campground and includes barrier-free toilets and showers, as well as close proximity to shuttle stops.
  • The free on-site festival shuttle runs all weekend, is wheelchair accessible, and stops near the Fußgängerbrücke (Footbridge), close to accessible camping, Glamping, and Utopia Camp.
  • Bassliner shuttle routes from Dessau and Gräfenhainichen are wheelchair accessible. For all other routes, foldable wheelchairs can usually be placed in the luggage compartment, depending on space availability. We recommend contacting Bassliner Support before booking to confirm arrangements.
  • If you have a disability card and have purchased a ticket, you’re welcome to write to us — we’re happy to extend one free companion pass to your accompanying person to help you out.
  • Please note that some beaches and sandy areas are not fully wheelchair accessible, and assistance dogs (as well as all animals) are not permitted due to high sound levels.
  • For any access-related questions, please contact access@wholefestival.com. Reaching out by June is especially helpful so we have enough time to prepare and accommodate you properly.
  • For more info on the topic of Accessibility, please see our FAQ.

03Utopia — Rent Camping Gear

  • We partner with Utopia to offer a more sustainable way to camp. Instead of producing new tents, Utopia rescues, repairs, and reuses quality tents left behind at other festivals.
  • Rent tents and basic camping gear on site
  • Skip traveling with bulky equipment
  • Eco-friendly approach focused on reuse
  • Limited availability, so book through Utopia now


    Utopia Bookings: Transfers & Cancellations

    Update: June 18 - We are looking to see if Utopia can be added to Tixel, our official resale partner. Check back for more updates.

Can I transfer my Utopia booking to a friend?

Yes! If you want to pass your booking along to a friend, please follow these steps:

Email Utopia: Send an email to info@utopia-camping.com and make sure to CC your friend.

Provide details: Clearly state that you want to transfer the booking and include your friend's full name.

Confirm the deposit rule: Because payment details cannot be changed in the system, the security deposit will always be refunded to the original buyer's account. Your friend must reply to the email thread confirming they understand and accept this.

Once everyone confirms, Utopia will update the booking, issue a new confirmation and QR code to your friend, and deactivate the old one.

Can I sell my booking to someone I don't know?

Utopia strongly advises against selling or transferring your booking to a stranger.

Because the system cannot change the original payment details, the security deposit will always be automatically refunded to the original buyer. This creates a high risk for financial disputes between buyers and sellers that Utopia cannot manage or resolve.

I can no longer attend. Can I cancel my booking?

If the booking is still eligible for a cancellation under Utopia's terms, it can be cancelled to return the tent to the inventory.

Please note that for high-demand events (like WHOLE), cancelled tents automatically go back onto Utopia's website for anyone to book. Utopia cannot "hold" or reserve a cancelled tent for a specific person.

01AWARENESS

Awareness Team & Support

  • An expanded Awareness Team made up of trained professionals is present throughout the festival
  • First Aid and paramedics are on-site at all times
  • An on-call psychologist is available for escalations and acute situations
  • Awareness Tents and support points are clearly marked on the festival map and app
  • If you or someone near you isn’t feeling well, physically or emotionally, seek help early. Don’t isolate in a tent or assume it will pass. Asking for support is part of participating responsibly.


Partying With Care

WHOLE is built on shared responsibility. We gather to experience pleasure, freedom, and connection , and that only works when care is practiced collectively.

Our Awareness & Safety framework exists to support everyone on site, especially those from communities that are most often asked to carry risk elsewhere. Care is not an afterthought here; it is infrastructure.

Looking Out for One Another

Check in if someone seems disoriented, overwhelmed, or unsafe

Step in when something doesn’t feel right, or alert staff immediately

If you’re unsure, err on the side of care — our team is here to help, not judge

Harm Reduction & Personal Responsibility

  • Stay hydrated. Eat regularly. Rest when needed.
  • Pace yourself — this is a marathon, not a sprint.
  • As in any other club or public setting, you are expected to act responsibly and discreetly, respecting both people and place. Please be mindful of your surroundings — the festival isn’t a total free-for-all.
  • Reckless behavior puts yourself and others at risk and may result in removal from the site.
  • If you require refrigerated medication, please visit the Info Point upon arrival.


02COMMUNITY GUIDELINES

How We Share This Space

WHOLE is a collective experience. These guidelines aren’t abstract values, this is the bare minimum that we come to expect from one another. Care is not something provided to you, it is something we practice with together.

WHOLE exists because queer communities have always known how to gather, protect, and imagine together. This weekend is no different.

GIVE WAY

  • Make space for others, especially those from marginalized communities
  • Be aware of how much space you take up — physically, emotionally, and socially
  • Kindness and consideration are non-negotiable

ASK, DON’T ASSUME

  • Respect names, pronouns, and boundaries
  • Do not assume identity, background, desire, or access needs
  • What feels casual to you may feel unsafe or triggering to someone else

CONSENT

  • Consent is active, mutual, and enthusiastic
  • It applies to touch, conversation, photography, and sexual interaction
  • It can be withdrawn at any time
  • No explanation is required

If you witness or experience a violation of consent, contact the Awareness Team or a staff member immediately.

SET AN INTENTION

Festivals can be intense environments. Arriving with intention helps sustain the care we expect from one another.

  • Take time to ground yourself
  • Know your limits
  • Set personal boundaries and check in with yourself often.
  • Respect the boundaries of others

WHOLE has zero tolerance for:

  • Harassment, discrimination, or violence
  • Racism, Islamophobia, antisemitism, transphobia, homophobia, misogyny, ableism, or fatphobia
  • Aggressive, threatening, or abusive behavior
  • Reckless behavior that puts yourself and others at risk
